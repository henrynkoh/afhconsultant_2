## Module Breakdown: What You'll Learn

### Module 1: Setting Up Your Home
**WAC References**: 388-76-10525, 10585, 10584, 10885, 10890, 10320, 10325, 10355

Master the physical plant requirements that turn a house into a compliant AFH:
- Exterior & Entry: Ramps (1:12 ratio), handrails, doorways
- Interior Safety: Fire systems, water temperature, environmental controls
- Resident Bedrooms: Square footage calculations, egress windows, privacy
- Kitchen & Sanitation: Cross-contamination prevention, storage
- Administrative Records: 23-tab binder system, mandatory postings

**Key Learning**: Understanding the "why" behind each requirement helps you provide better guidance to providers.

### Module 2: Emergency Planning
**WAC References**: 388-76-10830 through 10855

Prepare providers for any emergency situation:
- Master Disaster Plan: Written plan addressing natural and man-made disasters
- 72-Hour Survival Logistics: Food, water, and supply calculations
- Medical Readiness: First aid supplies and training requirements
- Drill Mastery: Partial and full evacuation procedures

**Key Learning**: The 5-minute evacuation standard isn't just a number—it's a life-saving requirement that requires careful planning and practice.

### Module 3: Medication Systems & Documentation
**WAC References**: 388-76-10430 through 10490

Master the critical area of medication management:
- System Setup: Requirements before resident admission
- Assistance Levels: Independent, self-admin with assistance, administration
- Storage & Safety: Locked storage, label integrity, alteration orders
- Refusals & Disposal: 30-day disposal rule, documentation requirements

**Key Learning**: Understanding the difference between "assistance" and "administration" is crucial for compliance and resident safety.

### Module 4: MAR Documentation
**WAC References**: 388-76-10430 through 10490

Learn to read and fill out Medication Administration Records correctly:
- Decoding MARs: Understanding all components
- Transcription: Moving orders from practitioners to MARs
- Filling Out MARs: Initials rule, error correction, signature keys
- Managing Refusals: Documentation protocols and practitioner notification

**Key Learning**: The MAR is a legal document. Every entry matters, and errors must be handled correctly—no white-out, no erasures.

### Module 5: Nutrition & Activities
**WAC References**: 388-112A, 388-76-10415

Ensure providers meet nutritional and activity requirements:
- Nutritional Foundation: Three-meal mandate, snack requirements, safe handling
- Resident-Centered Dining: Cultural accommodation, input in planning
- Social Activities: Person-centered engagement, specialized activities for dementia

**Key Learning**: Compliance isn't just about rules—it's about dignity, choice, and quality of life for residents.

## The R.A.R. Method: How Consultants Think

Every effective consultant uses the R.A.R. Method:

**R - Regulation (WAC)**: What does the code literally say?
**A - Assessment (Risk)**: What is the danger to a vulnerable adult?
**R - Remedy (Physical)**: What exact tool or measurement fixes it?

This platform teaches you to apply this method to every compliance question, ensuring you can provide logical, well-reasoned guidance.
